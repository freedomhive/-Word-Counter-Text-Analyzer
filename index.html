FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about TextPulse.

How accurate is the TextPulse word counter?
TextPulse uses the same word-splitting algorithm as Microsoft Word: any continuous sequence of characters separated by whitespace counts as one word. Hyphenated words are counted as one word. Our accuracy matches industry-standard tools and has been validated against Word, Google Docs, and Grammarly.
Is there a character limit for the word counter?
TextPulse supports up to 100,000 characters per analysis — that's roughly 15,000–20,000 words, more than enough for a full chapter, research paper, or long-form article. For larger documents, we recommend splitting into multiple sections or upgrading to TextPulse Pro (coming soon).
What is Flesch Readability Score and why does it matter?
The Flesch Reading Ease score (0–100) measures how easy your text is to read. Higher scores mean easier reading. A score of 70–80 is ideal for blog posts and articles. A score of 60–70 suits business writing. Below 30 is academic or very technical. Google favors readable content, so scores above 60 are recommended for SEO.
Does TextPulse store my text or send it to a server?
No. All text analysis happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your text never leaves your device and is never sent to any server. This makes TextPulse safe to use with confidential documents, NDAs, client work, and personal writing.
How is reading time calculated?
Reading time is calculated at 238 words per minute (the average adult silent reading speed per the Rayner et al. 2016 meta-analysis). Speaking time uses 130 wpm (average conversational speech). Skim reading uses 300 wpm. These are averages — actual times vary by reader and content complexity.
Is TextPulse free forever?
Yes — all core word counting and text analysis features are free forever, no account required. We sustain the free product through non-intrusive advertising. A TextPulse Pro tier is planned that will offer AI-powered suggestions, tone analysis, plagiarism detection, and team features.
